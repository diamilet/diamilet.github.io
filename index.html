Hi, I’m Diamy! Welcome to my webpage.

I'm a Postdoctoral Associate at the Extreme Events Institute's International Hurricane Research Center at Florida International University. In 2019, I received my Ph.D. degree in Atmospheric Science from the Program in Atmospheres, Oceans, and Climate at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. At MIT, I worked with Prof. Kerry Emanuel on the formation and maintenance of tropical cyclone - or hurricane - rain bands. My primary focus is to better understand the physics of tropical cyclones, which are dangerous storms that annually affect many people around the world. I was born and raised in the island of Puerto Rico, where I experienced the threat of tropical cyclones at an early age. I decided to study them with the aim of helping vulnerable communities achieve better preparedness. Before graduate school, I earned my B.S. in Theoretical Physics from the University of Puerto Rico at Mayagüez.
